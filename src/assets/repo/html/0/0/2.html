Here, we are concerned with a particular set of Ramsey numbers: those for R(3,k) where k is arbitrary. Erdos spent much of his career analyzing the asymptotics of R(3,k), and in 1961 discovered the lower bound R(3,k) = Ω((k / ln k)2 using the probabalistic method. It took until 1995 to prove the corresponding upper bound, which was discovered by Jeong-Han Kim. However, we are not here concerned with the general asymptotics of R(3,k), but rather the derivation of an upper bound on one of its instances.
