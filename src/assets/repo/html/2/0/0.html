    Step (a) → (b) is justified because, in the summation of all neighborhoods, every vertex of degree d will be counted exactly d times- once for each of its neighbors. So, each vertex of degree d contributes (d2) to the sum, and there are nd vertices of degree d.

    Step (b) → (c) is justified because, in the summation, the structure of G is irrelevant. All that matters is how many vertices there are of a particular degree, as the e(…) term will be identical for all vertices of identical degree.

    (4) is helpful because it allows us to calculate γ(G) from only the degree sequence of G, rather than having to look at each neighborhood in G itself. (4) is referenced in several of the proofs below.
