For any graphs G = (V,E) and v ∈ V

  • e(G) is the number of edges in G
  • degv(G) is the degree of v
  • δ(G) is the minimum degree of any vertex in G
  • Δ(G) is the maximum degree of any vertex in G
  • Gv is the graph induced by V - ({neighbors of v}∪{v})
  • γ(v) = e(G) - Z(v) - e(3,k - 1;n - deg(v) - 1) ≥ 0

    In other words, γ(v) is the difference between the actual value of Z(v) and the lowest possible value of Z(v) in a graph of that type. The requirement that γ(v) is greater than 0 is just a statement that Z(v) must be at least as large as this lower bound.
